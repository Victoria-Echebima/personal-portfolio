ABOUT ME

I'm Victoria Echebima, born on April 23, 2002, in Benin City, Edo State, Nigeria, with roots in Egbu, Owerri North L.G.A of Imo State. I was raised in a supportive family that instilled in me the values of hard work, faith, education, and service to others. These values shaped my early path into nursing, where I found a deep sense of purpose in caring for people. For me, healthcare has always been more than a profession—it's a calling driven by empathy, compassion, and a genuine desire to make a meaningful difference in people's lives.

As I grew in my career, I developed a strong interest in technology and problem-solving, which led me to explore web development. I became fascinated by how digital tools can transform healthcare delivery, improve access to information, and support better patient outcomes. This inspired my goal of building health tech solutions that bridge the gap between care and technology. Balancing nursing with learning full stack development has been challenging, but my determination keeps me going. I'm motivated by the vision of creating platforms and applications that can simplify healthcare processes and positively impact lives on a larger scale.

Outside of my professional journey, I enjoy simple, meaningful experiences that help me relax and express myself. I believe in continuous growth and self-improvement, and I'm driven by the idea that it's never too late to evolve or pursue a bigger vision. Learning web development is not just a career shift for me—it's a step toward building innovative health tech solutions, expanding my impact, and shaping a future that reflects both my passion for helping others and my curiosity for innovation.
